(1) Anonymity

What is Anonymity? ×
Anonymity refers to the state of being not identifiable within a set of subjects. It is often used to protect user privacy in various online and offline scenarios.
According to China Youth Daily, the survey conducted among 3,620 college students from 175 universities highlights significant concerns about the relationship between anonymity and cyber violence. A majority of respondents believe that anonymity facilitates the spread of false information (66.46%) and makes it challenging to hold perpetrators accountable in fraudulent activities (64.17%). Additionally, over half the students expressed concerns about increased risks of encountering harmful individuals and the proliferation of violent and pornographic content due to anonymity. These findings emphasize the urgent need for strategies to mitigate the risks associated with anonymous environments on the internet.

(2) Moderation

What is Moderation? ×
Moderation involves the regulation and control of content to ensure it adheres to community guidelines and legal standards, often used in digital platforms to maintain a safe and respectful environment.

(3) Divided and Rules

What is divided and rules? ×
In politics and sociology, gaining and maintaining power divisively. This includes the exploitation of existing divisions within a political group by its political opponents, and also the deliberate creation or strengthening of such divisions.

The strategy of "divide and rule" has been extensively employed globally since ancient times. This tactic involves creating or exploiting divisions among populations to prevent them from uniting against a common adversary, thereby making it easier to maintain control over them. This approach has been documented as early as the first century BC and has been a recurrent method in the political and military strategies of various empires and leaders throughout history to consolidate power and control.

Nation-State Cyberattacks

Divide and Rule Application: Governments engage in these attacks not only to harm their adversaries directly but also to sow discord within the targeted nation. For instance, cyberattacks that leak sensitive information can lead to public distrust in the government, fragmenting societal unity and weakening the country internally.

Cyber Terrorism

Divide and Rule Application: Acts of cyber terrorism are designed to create fear and chaos. By targeting critical infrastructure and spreading propaganda, these acts can exacerbate divisions within a society. This makes it easier for the perpetrating entity to manipulate public opinion and control the narrative, thus maintaining power or destabilizing a government.

Sanctions and Cyber Retaliation

Divide and Rule Application: By imposing sanctions or retaliating against cyber adversaries, a government can not only punish those directly responsible but also send a signal to other potential perpetrators. This can lead to divisions among enemy ranks, as individuals or groups may begin to act more cautiously or even turn against each other to avoid similar repercussions.

Cyberbullying and Harassment

Divide and Rule Application: When powerful individuals or public figures are targeted, the harassment can serve to discredit them and diminish their influence. This tactic can be used by political opponents to divide public opinion and distract from more significant issues. It can also polarize the public, with some defending the harassed individuals and others condemning them based on the propagated narratives.

The persistence of cyberbullying is fueled by the anonymity afforded by digital platforms, which allows perpetrators to operate without accountability. The vast reach and accessibility of the internet enable rapid and widespread dissemination of harmful content. Moreover, those who engage in cyberbullying often possess advanced technological skills, allowing them to manipulate digital interactions effectively. This imbalance not only makes it difficult for victims to seek redress but also challenges regulatory efforts to control or mitigate such behavior.

Overall, the United States, as the foremost superpower, registers the highest rates of cyberbullying globally. This trend is attributed to the extensive use of social media and digital communication platforms among Americans, coupled with a highly competitive societal framework that often glorifies aggressive behaviors and conflict. Such dynamics not only perpetuate existing cyberbullying issues but also complicate efforts to implement effective preventive measures and educational programs aimed at reducing the incidence of online harassment and abuse.
